IMPORTANT BUSINESS RULES
1. A Job Profile can be linked to either:
   - a Task Grade, or
   - an Upper Limit
   depending on the Employee Type rules.
2. A Position is linked to a Job Profile.
3. An Employee is linked to a Position.
4. The employee’s salary source must come from the Job Profile / Position chain, using the correct Task Grade or Upper Limit linked to that Job Profile.
5. This is critical:
   - Do NOT let the system mix Task Grade and Upper Limit incorrectly.
   - The correct salary source must always be determined from the employee’s linked position -> job profile.

5. HOURS AND RATES CALCULATIONS
==================================================

Populate the Hours and Rates tab using:
- salary from Task Grade or Upper Limit
- working hours per month from Employee Type defaults
- working days per month from Employee Type defaults

Calculations:
1. Monthly Fixed Salary
   - from the correct salary source (Task Grade or Upper Limit)

2. Annual Salary
   - Monthly Fixed Salary * 12

3. Working Hours Per Day
   - Working Hours Per Month / Working Days Per Month
   - round sensibly to 2 decimals unless existing payroll standard differs

4. Rate Per Day
   - Monthly Fixed Salary / Working Days Per Month

5. Rate Per Hour
   - Monthly Fixed Salary / Working Hours Per Month

Validation:
- prevent divide by zero
- if working hours or working days are missing or zero, show validation/warning and do not crash
- read-only calculated fields should not be manually editable unless current design specifically requires overrides
