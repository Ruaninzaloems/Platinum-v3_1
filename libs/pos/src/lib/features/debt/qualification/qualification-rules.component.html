What are Qualification Rules?

Qualification Rules define which debtor accounts qualify for debt recovery actions (Section 129 notices, handovers, legal proceedings). Each rule contains conditions that filter accounts based on their arrears, payment behaviour, and property information. Only accounts matching ALL conditions in a rule will be flagged for action.

Step 1 — Create a New Rule

  1. Click the + Add Rule button at the top right
  2. Enter a Rule Name — use a descriptive name like "High-value water debtors over 90 days"
  3. Add an optional Description to explain what the rule targets
  4. Set a Priority number — higher numbers are evaluated first. Use this when multiple rules may apply to the same account

Step 2 — Add Conditions

Each rule needs at least one condition. Conditions follow the pattern: FIELD OPERATOR VALUE

Available FieldWhat It ChecksExample Use
Total Arrears (R)Combined outstanding amount across all services> 5000 (over R5,000 in arrears)
Water Arrears (R)Outstanding water charges only> 2000 (water debt over R2,000)
Electricity Arrears (R)Outstanding electricity charges only> 1000
Rates Arrears (R)Outstanding municipal rates> 3000
Refuse Arrears (R)Outstanding refuse/solid waste charges> 500
Sewerage Arrears (R)Outstanding sewerage charges> 500
Arrear Age (days)How many days the oldest arrear has been outstanding> 90 (overdue more than 90 days)
Days Since Last PaymentNumber of days since the account last made a payment> 60 (no payment in 2 months)
Property Value (R)Valuation amount of the property> 500000 (high-value properties)
Risk Score (0-100)Score from the Risk Scoring module> 60 (high risk accounts)
Indigent StatusWhether the account is registered as indigent= false (exclude indigents)
Previous Legal ActionsCount of past legal actions against the account= 0 (first-time offenders only)
Debt Size (R)Total debt owed (same as total arrears)>= 10000
Payment History ScoreScore reflecting past payment behaviour (0-100)< 30 (poor payment history)
Location Risk ScoreArea-based risk factor (0-100)> 70

Step 3 — Combine Conditions with AND / OR

Add multiple conditions by clicking + Add Condition. Choose how they combine:

  • AND — All conditions must be true. Example: Total Arrears > 5000 AND Arrear Age > 90 means the account must owe more than R5,000 and be overdue for more than 90 days.
  • OR — At least one condition must be true. Example: Water Arrears > 3000 OR Electricity Arrears > 3000 means either water or electricity debt qualifies.
Example: Identify accounts for Section 129 Notice
WHERE Total Arrears (R) > 5000
AND Arrear Age (days) > 90
AND Indigent Status = false
AND Days Since Last Payment > 60

This rule targets non-indigent accounts owing more than R5,000 that are 90+ days overdue and haven't paid in 2 months.

Example: High-value property debtors
WHERE Property Value (R) > 800000
AND Total Arrears (R) > 10000
AND Previous Legal Actions = 0

Targets high-value properties with significant debt that haven't been through legal action before.

Step 4 — Test Your Rule

  1. Scroll down to the Test Accounts Data section
  2. Enter test account data as CSV lines, one per line. The format is:
    accountNo, totalArrears, arrearDays, lastPaymentDays, propertyValue, waterArrears, electricityArrears
  3. Click the ▶ play button on your rule in the table
  4. The results will show how many accounts matched and which ones qualify
Test data example
ACC001, 15000, 120, 90, 800000, 5000, 3000
ACC002, 3000, 30, 15, 250000, 1000, 500
ACC003, 8500, 95, 70, 450000, 4000, 2000
ACC004, 500, 10, 5, 300000, 200, 100

With the Section 129 rule above, ACC001 and ACC003 would match. ACC002 has too few arrear days and ACC004's arrears are too low.

Step 5 — Manage Your Rules

  • Toggle Active/Inactive — Use the switch in the table to enable or disable a rule without deleting it
  • Edit — Click the ✎ pencil icon to modify an existing rule
  • Delete — Click the 🗑 trash icon to permanently remove a rule
  • Priority Order — When multiple rules apply, higher priority rules are evaluated first

Quick Tips

  • Start with a simple rule (e.g., Total Arrears > 5000) and add conditions gradually
  • Always test your rule with sample data before relying on it for debt recovery
  • Use the Indigent Status = false condition to exclude indigent households from legal action
  • Create separate rules for different service types (water-specific, electricity-specific) to manage service-level disconnections
  • Rules with higher priority numbers run first — use this to catch critical accounts before general rules
